Compensation Social

Video Game Soundtrack

2021 - 2023

Role: Composition & Mixing/Mastering

Compensation Social was my first time ever working on a soundtrack. I had made some music for personal purposes before, but this was my first time writing music that had to match a set theme and environment. By working on Compensation Social I not only learnt the aforementioned skills to write music for an environment, but I also learnt how to invoke a certain emotion in the listener. If you'd like to listen to the OSTs you can click on the links for Volume 1, Volume 2, and Volume 3.

BYTE

Video Game Soundtrack

2023 - 2025

Role: Composition & Mixing/Mastering

Work on BYTE started soon after I played ULTRAKILL and realized my passion for movement shooters. The OST for BYTE was a big step for me in learning how to properly mix and master music for video games as well as advance my skills in writing music that fits environments. Whilst working on the soundtrack I also learnt how to collaborate on songs with other musicians, as heard on "YOU'RE NOT WELCOME HERE", but also other pieces of the soundtrack that were unfortunately never published. The full OST is available on YouTube.

Personal Music

Independent Work

2023 - Present

Since 2023, I have been writing music under my own title, which I have been distributing to streaming services since early 2024 with the release of MiniLog and Synthesizer Cat. Since then I have also branched out into other directions with my music, such as rock, experimental, cinematic music and more. One of my most notable releases is I Saw The Sky Tear, my first ever rock album. As well as Tales Of The Forgotten, which is an instrumental concept album about a girl falling through the depths of the earth, space and time, and eventually floating through an empty universe. Though in my time I have also done collaborations, like the single The Prison Planet, Celestia. All of my music is available on the most popular streaming platforms, so I'd encourage checking out my catalog of almost 60 songs.
